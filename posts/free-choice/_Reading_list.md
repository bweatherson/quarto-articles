## The Connection Between NPIs and Free Choice

The relationship between negative polarity items (NPIs) and free choice items (FCIs) is central to understanding both free choice permission and ability modals. In English, "any" functions as both an NPI and an FCI depending on context, which makes it particularly important for these debates.

### Key Work on NPIs/FCIs in Permission and Modal Contexts

**1. Maria Aloni (2007) - "Free choice, modals, and imperatives"**

This seminal paper proposes an analysis of imperatives and possibility and necessity statements that explains their differences with respect to the licensing of free choice "any" and accounts for the related phenomena of free choice disjunction in imperatives, permissions, and statements. "Any" and "or" are analyzed as operators introducing sets of alternative propositions, with free choice licensing operators treated as quantifiers over these sets.

Aloni specifically addresses why:
- "You may talk to anyone" (permission modal) licenses FCI "any" with free choice reading
- "Vincent must be anywhere" (necessity modal) does not license FCI "any"
- Imperatives pattern with permission modals in licensing FCI "any"

**2. Anastasia Giannakidou's work on nonveridicality**

Giannakidou develops a framework where NPIs are licensed in nonveridical contexts (where truth is not entailed), which includes questions, modals, and conditionals. Modal environments are known to be non-monotone, and both NPIs and FCIs are licensed in modal contexts. There is a class of items known as free choice items which require typically modal and quantificational licensers.

This explains why:
- "John may talk to anybody" is grammatical (permission modal licenses NPI/FCI)
- "Press any key" (imperative) is grammatical

**3. The "any" puzzle for ability modals**

Modal verbs like "can" create nonveridical contexts that license "any," and ability modals specifically pattern with other modals in licensing NPIs/FCIs. However, the interaction is complex because ability modals have special properties.
